Magnus Carlsen 

{The Chess Prodigy}

Info

Became a chess prodigy: earned GM title at just 13 years, 148 days (one of the youngest ever).
In 2010, at age 19, became world no. 1 in FIDE rankings.
In 2013, defeated Viswanathan Anand to become World Chess Champion.
Successfully defended the title against Anand (2014), Sergey Karjakin (2016), Fabiano Caruana (2018), and Ian Nepomniachtchi (2021).
In 2023, he chose not to defend his world championship title, but remains the highest-rated player in the world.

Achievements

Classical World Chess Champion (2013–2023)
World Rapid Chess Champion (multiple times)
World Blitz Chess Champion (multiple times)
Winner of the FIDE World Cup (first FIDE World Cup win in 2023).

Honors

Chess Oscar — awarded the “best player” by Russian chess magazine 64 several times (2009-2013).
Peer Gynt Prize (2011) — a major Norwegian cultural award for outstanding achievement in society.
Named one of Time magazine’s 100 Most Influential People in the World (2013).
VG “Name of the Year” (Årets navn) — a Norwegian news award (2009, 2013).
Dagbladet People’s Choice “Sportsman of the Year” several times (e.g. 2009, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2016).
